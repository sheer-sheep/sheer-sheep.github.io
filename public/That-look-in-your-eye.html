That Look in your Eye

Today a woman arrived at my concert hall and before she was completely in, I could hear her smile echoed the walls, resounding with self-Reflection and growth. when I trumpeted for her name, Lucia, floated through the air like a Clef from the page. Her reason for being there, she sang, was that before her passing she wanted to reexperience her life through song.

With passion in my heart, I belted a powerful declaration that she was in the right place if she wanted to witness her life's song.

As we conversed, Lucia described the events of her troublesome upbringing. How her parents’ dissonance owever, some were jealous and dampened her sweet tones. Lucia then struggled with self-confidence and noticed the cadence in her life was a series of lost love. A chord was struck deep within me when she described that for her to raise her newborn as a single mother, she had to overcome the coda that was her troublesome youth and raise them with a brighter chorus than she had.

As I listened to her, I collected her tears and tales and strung them around the page as notes and chords, like a weaver spinning silk. Lucia went on to describe how she led her maturing daughter to harmonize with the world, and how to be a part of the symphony of everyday life. Lucia witnessed the happiness her daughter had as she found success in love and played it to everyone to hear. Life had passed its crescendo for and having rode the rising and falling waves of intonation that life had for her, she felt it was time to capture it. Having heard Lucia’s musical number I understood what brought her here and the piece she wanted me to craft for her.

With a somber and warm tone, I bid Lady Lucia farewell. Behind my closed-door passerby’s would hear Clashing of symbols as I played the Confliction of her parents. Crows would hear the rise and fall of violin reeds producing a vibrato of life and rebirth. And finally with the passing of her daughter, the ensemble within her halls produced a loud roar that’ll rush out of the streets exclaiming crushing sadness and the love of life and new beginnings. In silence ,in the street’s hearts stopped, and with tears in the world’s eyes, everyone resonated a love and understanding for the song of Lady Lucia.
